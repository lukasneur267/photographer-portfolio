About Me

I'm Elena Marchetti, and I shoot weddings, couples, travel, and portraits between Italy and Vienna. I trained in Milan, started out photographing family celebrations and small editorial jobs, and slowly built my way into destination weddings across Europe, the Amalfi Coast, the Austrian Alps, wherever the work takes me. Somewhere along the way I stopped thinking of myself as someone who documents events and started thinking of myself as someone who collects moments people actually want to keep.

I'm not interested in the pose. I'm after what's underneath it. People forget the camera is there eventually, and that's when something true shows up: a glance, a laugh mid-sentence, a hand reaching for someone else's. A wedding day isn't a performance to me. It's a chapter I've been trusted to remember, and I take that seriously, even when I'm just standing quietly in a corner with a long lens, waiting.

My work has run in regional wedding and travel publications, and in 2022 the Vienna Portrait Awards recognized my ongoing series on long-married couples, a project I started almost by accident after photographing my grandparents' fiftieth anniversary. I still shoot film when the moment calls for it, switching between color and black and white depending on what the story actually needs. Some days that means grain and shadow. Other days it means getting out of the way entirely and letting the color do the talking.

I'm based in Vienna now, still taking on weddings, portraits, and travel work across Europe. I've shot in barns, ballrooms, and on the side of a mountain in the rain, and none of those settings change what I'm actually after: a real moment, honestly seen.
